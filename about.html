About Me

I am a data scientist located in the Washington, D.C. metro area currently working as a senior consultant at MorganFranklin.

I was born and raised in Houston, Texas, but after graduating from high school I enrolled at Penn State to study industrial engineering, which turned out to be my first step in the direction of becoming a data scientist. I learned fundamental data science disciplines, such as statistical modeling and operations research.

After graduating in 2015, I moved to D.C. to work for ICF where I was a consultant for the Department of Housing and Urban Development. During most of my time with ICF, my responsibilities included cost estimation for both large and small scale information technology infrastructure changes.

In my free time, I enjoy learning new things, by constantly reading books and listening to podcasts, and seeing new places, whether it's a park or museum in my own city or a far away country that offers breathtaking sunsets and spectacular waves. I am happiest when I am near the ocean, be it on the beach gazing out at the glimmering water or on a surfboard riding the waves. Additionally, I am a health freak who loves going above and beyond with eating habits and exercise routines in order to be the healthiest and happiest human I can be.
